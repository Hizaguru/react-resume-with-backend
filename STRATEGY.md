### WHY A SOLO CONSULTANT (NOT AN AGENCY)

**Section goal**: Proactively address the biggest objection: "Shouldn't I hire a company with more people?"

**Headline**: Why hire one developer instead of an agency?

**Body**:

Agencies sell you a team. You get a project manager, a designer, a frontend developer, a backend developer, and a QA engineer. Sounds comprehensive — until you realize:

- **You're paying for coordination, not code.** Half the budget goes to people managing other people.
- **Nobody owns the full picture.** The frontend developer doesn't understand the backend. The backend developer has never seen the deployment config.
- **Communication gets filtered.** Your feedback passes through a project manager before reaching the person who can actually act on it.

With Perttula Software, you get one senior developer who understands every layer of your system. Decisions are faster. Communication is direct. There's nowhere to hide and no one to blame.

**When an agency makes sense**: If your project genuinely requires 5+ specialists working simultaneously, hire an agency. For everything else — and that's most projects — one capable developer is faster, cheaper, and more accountable.

**Layout notes**: Two-column layout. Left side: the text. Right side: simple comparison visual (solo vs agency) or just clean typography. Light background.

## 6. Visual / Branding Direction

### Color Palette
- **Primary background**: Near-black (#0A0A0A or #111111) — premium, modern
- **Secondary background**: Dark gray (#1A1A1A) for alternating sections
- **Light sections**: Off-white (#F5F5F5) for contrast sections
- **Accent color**: Sharp blue (#2563EB) or teal (#0D9488) — move away from orange which reads "portfolio template"
- **Text primary**: White (#FFFFFF) on dark, near-black (#111111) on light
- **Text secondary**: Medium gray (#9CA3AF)
- **Accent hover**: Lighter shade of accent

### Typography
- **Headings**: Inter, Geist, or Instrument Sans — clean, geometric, modern
- **Body**: Same family at regular weight, or a complementary sans-serif
- **Code/tech labels**: JetBrains Mono or similar monospace
- **Sizing**: Large headlines (48-64px), generous line height, tight letter spacing on headings

### Spacing
- Generous whitespace between sections (120-160px padding)
- Tight content blocks within sections
- Max content width ~1200px, centered
- Consistent vertical rhythm

### Imagery Style
- No stock photos of people at laptops
- Subtle geometric patterns or grid textures for backgrounds
- Clean screenshots of actual work for portfolio
- Dark mode first — light sections for contrast rhythm
- No gradients unless very subtle

### Overall Feel
- Minimal, dark, typographic
- Feels like a developer built it (intentionally)
- Clean enough for business clients, technical enough to signal competence
- Think: Linear, Vercel, Raycast — that aesthetic tier

## 8. Optional Improvements for Trust and Conversion

1. **Get a business domain email**: contact@perttulasoftware.fi reads more professional than outlook.com
2. **Real testimonials**: Even one real quote from a satisfied client changes everything. Ask past collaborators.
3. **Case study detail pages**: For your best 2-3 projects, write a one-page breakdown: problem, approach, outcome.
4. **Add a blog or insights section**: Short technical posts show expertise and help SEO. Not required at launch.
5. **Pricing signals**: You don't need public prices, but phrases like "Projects typically start at..." reduce tire-kicker inquiries.
6. **Response time commitment**: "I respond within one business day" builds trust.
7. **LinkedIn profile alignment**: Make sure your LinkedIn matches the new positioning.
8. **Business registration visibility**: If Perttula Software is a registered Finnish business (toiminimi/oy), mention it subtly in the footer — it signals legitimacy.
9. **Cookie/privacy notice**: Required under Finnish/EU law for a business site.
10. **Finnish language version**: Consider a bilingual site if targeting local Finnish businesses.

## Copy Tips — Making It Feel Like a Consultancy

- Never say "passionate" or "enthusiastic" — let the work speak
- Use "I" not "we" — honest about being solo, which is the selling point
- Talk about client outcomes, not your feelings about code
- Use specific numbers when possible ("delivered in 6 weeks", "reduced load time by 40%")
- Avoid exclamation marks — confidence doesn't need volume
- Keep paragraphs to 2-3 sentences max
- Every section should answer: "Why should the visitor keep scrolling?"
- The site should feel like it was built by someone who builds sites for a living
